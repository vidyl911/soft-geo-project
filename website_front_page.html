PolluTrack

Why Should We Track Pollution?

Pollution, especially air pollution, poses serious threats to human health and the environment. Fine particles and harmful gases are known to cause respiratory diseases, cardiovascular issues, and premature death. Monitoring these pollutants helps in making informed policy decisions, protecting public health, and identifying pollution sources. Understanding trends in air quality empowers communities, governments, and industries to act responsibly and proactively.

Key Pollutants We Monitor

Carbon Monoxide (CO)

A colorless, odorless gas from incomplete combustion of fossil fuels. It interferes with oxygen transport in the blood, causing fatigue, headaches, and even death at high concentrations.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2)

A reddish-brown gas from fossil fuel combustion, especially in vehicles. It irritates lungs, reduces immunity, and contributes to ground-level ozone and smog formation.

Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)

A group of gases including NO and NO2 produced by vehicles, power plants, and industry. NOx gases contribute to acid rain, ground-level ozone, and respiratory issues.

Ozone (O3)

A reactive gas formed when sunlight reacts with NOx and VOCs. Ground-level ozone causes breathing problems, aggravates asthma, and damages crops and vegetation.

Particulate Matter 2.5 (PM2.5)

Fine particles smaller than 2.5 micrometers. They can lodge deep in the lungs and enter the bloodstream, increasing the risk of heart and lung diseases.

Particulate Matter 10 (PM10)

Inhalable particles with a diameter of 10 micrometers or smaller. Commonly arise from dust, construction, and industry, and can worsen respiratory conditions.
